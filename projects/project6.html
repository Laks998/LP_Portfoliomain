The Big Question

Can you actually feel your mind separate from your body using Virtual Reality?

I took René Descartes' 17th-century theory that "mind and body are separate" and built a VR experience to make people actually feel this separation. Turns out, fear is one hell of a philosophical teacher.

The Challenge

How do you make abstract philosophy feel real?

Abstract Theory

Cartesian dualism is just philosophical theory - how do you make it experiential?

Technical Skills

Had to learn Unity and C# programming from scratch to build the VR environment

Research Rigor

Combine philosophy, psychology, and technology while maintaining academic standards

The Discovery

What I learned from interviewing 8 people about their weirdest experiences

Fear is the Key

Everyone who experienced mind-body separation mentioned extreme emotions - especially fear and stress

100% of dissociation linked to intense emotions

Pop Culture Gets It

Movies like The Matrix, Avatar, and Divergent are billion-dollar proof that people accept mind-body separation

Cultural validation through media success

Height = Horror

Multiple participants mentioned fear of heights as triggering out-of-body sensations

Primary design direction identified

The Solution

A terrifyingly effective elevator experience

01

02

The Ascent

03

The Reveal

04

The Separation

What I Learned

Insights that could change how we study consciousness

VR as Philosophy Lab

Virtual reality can make abstract theories tangible and testable, opening new research methods

Emotion-Driven Design

Fear and stress are powerful tools for inducing altered states of consciousness

External Control Works

When users don't control their virtual movement, they feel more detached from their bodies

Duration Matters

Longer exposure leads to deeper immersion and stronger philosophical impact
