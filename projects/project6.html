Research Process

1
Literature Review
Studied Cartesian dualism, dissociation psychology, out-of-body experiences, and VR's impact on consciousness perception.
2
User Interviews (n=8)
Semi-structured interviews revealed that dissociation clustered around extreme emotional states, particularly fear.
3
Prototype Development
Built VR elevator environment in Unity, designed to trigger fear-induced dissociation through height and loss of control.
4
Pilot Testing & Iteration
Initial testing revealed need for improved realism, dynamic camera movement, and convincing height representation.
5
Final Testing (n=5)
Participants with fear of heights experienced refined VR environment, followed by detailed post-experience interviews.

The VR Experience

A carefully orchestrated sequence designed to trigger dissociative states through emotional intensity and environmental manipulation.

01
Confined Entry
Participants begin seated in a dimly lit, creaky elevator interior
02
Ascending Tension
Gradual rise with mechanical sounds and visual movement cues
03
Dramatic Reveal
Doors open to panoramic cityscape, revealing extreme height
04
External Control
Camera moves autonomously, simulating out-of-body movement
