Key Insights from Thematic Analysis

01

Visual Realism is Critical

High-quality graphics and realistic environments were consistently mentioned as essential for immersion. Users immediately disengage when visuals feel artificial.

"If the sound doesn't match the visual, I start imagining how the visual should look"
02

Emotion Drives Immersion

Strong emotions (fear, awe, excitement) make users forget their physical surroundings. Emotional intensity directly correlates with presence.

"I would wake up feeling like I was being choked... I couldn't move. It was very scary."
03

External Control Enhances Presence

Paradoxically, having the system move users without their input increased immersion by reducing the cognitive gap between virtual and real.

"The entity will correct me to urge my hand in the right direction... that made it more realistic"
04

Height Creates Visceral Response

Vertical space and height were powerful triggers. Users reported physical sensations even knowing they were safe.

"Motion plays a big role in immersion"
05

Audio-Visual Synchronization

Spatial audio perfectly matched to visual events was essential. Any mismatch broke immersion immediately.

"Gaming, especially with social interaction, can make the experience feel very real"
06

Real-World Environments Work Best

Users preferred realistic recreations of real places over fantastical environments for maximum believability.

"You could recreate real places and give users the feeling of being there. That would be immersive."

Experience Design

Designed an elevator experience that uses fear of heights as the emotional trigger, with carefully choreographed moments building from confined safety to overwhelming exposure.

Design Concept

An elevator journey that starts in a small, safe space and gradually reveals extreme height, using the contrast between confined and open space to maximize emotional impact.

Experience Flow

01. Establish Baseline

User sits in dimly lit elevator. Familiar, enclosed space creates initial comfort and grounding before the experience begins.

Design Decision: Match real chair with virtual seat for seamless physical-virtual alignment

02. Build Anticipation

Slow upward movement with mechanical sounds. Moving brick walls provide elevation cues without revealing destination.

Design Decision: Visual-vestibular conflict enhances height sensation through motion parallax

03. Dramatic Reveal

Doors open to 360-degree cityscape from extreme height. Sudden transition from confined to infinite space creates peak emotional moment.

Design Decision: Real city panorama photos for authentic depth perception and scale

04. Loss of Control

Camera autonomously moves into open air. User experiences out-of-body sensation as system takes control during peak fear.

Design Decision: Autonomous movement removes user agency, paradoxically increasing immersion
