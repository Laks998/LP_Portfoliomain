Sportscove

Redesigning a sports learning platform from the ground up—focusing on user flows, information architecture, and creating intuitive experiences

Context

Sportscove is a platform connecting sports learners with expert coaches. When I joined in March 2025, the product existed only as confusing wireframes. My role was to transform these into a coherent, user-centered experience—focusing primarily on UX rather than visual design.

Why was there a need for the complete redesign?

The existing wireframes had fundamental UX issues that would have led to poor user experiences and high drop-off rates.

01

Convoluted User Flows

02

03

No Design System

04

Accessibility Gaps

No consideration for inclusive design practices or diverse user needs

Since this was primarily a UX challenge, I focused on understanding user needs, simplifying flows, and creating a systematic foundation for the entire product.

Everything I did to make it a good app to its users!

Streamlined Booking Flow

Reduced from 8 steps to 4 steps

The original booking flow had users moving through 8 separate screens with redundant information entry and no clear sense of progress. I consolidated related inputs, removed unnecessary steps, and added a progress indicator.

Before: 8 steps, unclear progress, redundant data entry
After: 4 steps, clear progress bar, smart defaults
Impact: Expected 23% increase in booking completion

Guest Mode Introduction

Browse before committing

Users were forced to create an account before seeing any coaches or programs. I introduced a guest mode that lets users explore the platform freely, then seamlessly transition to registration when they're ready to book.

Problem solved: High bounce rate at sign-up wall
Expected impact: 40% increase in sign-ups

Comprehensive Design System

Consistency at scale

Built a complete design system from scratch with 60+ components, ensuring every designer and developer worked from the same source of truth. This reduced design inconsistencies and accelerated development.

Components created: 60+ reusable patterns
Impact: 60% reduction in design time for new features

Highlights of my design journey

Usability Testing

Conducted 10 moderated sessions with target users to validate the redesigned flows. Task completion rate improved from 85% to 96%, and average time-to-book decreased by 40%.

Iterative Refinement

Worked in weekly sprints with the development team, testing prototypes and incorporating feedback. This iterative approach caught issues early and reduced development rework by 30%.

Stakeholder Collaboration

Regular sync meetings with CEO and advisors ensured design decisions aligned with business goals while keeping user needs at the center.

QA Testing

Manually tested APKs to identify design inconsistencies and usability issues before launch. Logged 50+ bugs and design improvements for the development team.

Custom Animations

While my focus was UX, I also created custom animations for onboarding and micro-interactions to enhance the user experience and reinforce brand identity.

What I Learned

User flows are everything: No amount of visual polish can save a confusing user flow. Getting the UX right first is non-negotiable.

Design systems pay off: Spending time upfront to build a comprehensive design system saved exponentially more time later in the project.

Test early, test often: Usability testing with prototypes caught critical issues that would have been expensive to fix post-development.

Collaboration is key: Working closely with developers and stakeholders ensured designs were both feasible and aligned with business goals.
